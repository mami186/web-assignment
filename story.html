Sam, a software developer, was stuck on a bug that made his app crash right before a big demo. Hours of debugging led nowhere, and frustration was mounting. Just as he was about to give up, he noticed a single misplaced semicolon in the code—a tiny oversight causing the entire issue. Fixing it, the app ran flawlessly.

Relieved, Sam leaned back with a smile. "Sometimes, it's the smallest things," he thought, appreciating how even in coding, life’s greatest problems often have simple solutions.
